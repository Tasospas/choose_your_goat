Michaeel Jeffrey Jordan

Jordan was born at Cumberland Hospital in Fort Greene, Brooklyn, New York City, on February 17, 1963,[28] the son of bank employee Deloris (née Peoples) and equipment supervisor James R. Jordan Sr.[28][29] In 1968, he moved with his family to Wilmington, North Carolina.[30] Jordan attended Emsley A. Laney High School in Wilmington, where he highlighted his athletic career by playing basketball, baseball, and football. He tried out for the basketball varsity team during his sophomore year; at 5 feet 11 inches (1.80 m), he was deemed too short to play at that level. His taller friend Harvest Leroy Smith was the only sophomore to make the team

LeBron Raymone James

As a freshman, James averaged 21 points and 6 rebounds per game for the St. Vincent–St. Mary varsity basketball team.[20] The Fighting Irish went 27–0 en route to the Division III state title, making them the only boys high school team in Ohio to finish the season undefeated.[21] As a sophomore, James averaged 25.2 points and 7.2 rebounds, along with 5.8 assists and 3.8 steals per game.[22] For some home games during the season, St. Vincent–St. Mary played at the University of Akron's 5,492-seat Rhodes Arena to satisfy ticket demand from alumni, fans, as well as college and NBA scouts who wanted to see James play.[23] The Fighting Irish finished the season 26–1 and repeated as state champions.[20] For his outstanding play, James was named Ohio Mr. Basketball and selected to the USA Today All-USA First Team, becoming the first sophomore to do either.[20]

Kobe "Bean" Bryant

Bryant was born in Philadelphia, the youngest of three children and the only son of Pamela Cox Bryant and former NBA player Joe Bryant. He was also the maternal nephew of NBA player John "Chubby" Cox.His parents named him after the famous beef of Kobe, Japan, which they saw on a restaurant menu. His middle name, Bean, was derived from his father's nickname "Jellybean." Bryant's family was Catholic and he was brought up with this faith.

Accolades

Important awards and a brief team history

Chicago Bulls (1984-1998), Washigton Wizards (2001-2003)

Michael Jordan was drafted in the 1984 draft by the Chicago Bulls with the 3rd overall pick. His accolades during that period are: 6x NBA Champion, 6x NBA Finals MVP, 1x Defensive player of the year, 5x NBA MVP and 1x Rookie of the year. He was a 14x All Star and a 3x All-Star Game MVP. He was also selected in 10 All-NBA First Teams and an 1 All-NBA Second team.

Cleveland Cavaliers (2003-2010, 2014-2018), Miami Heat (2010 - 2014), Los Angeles Lakers (2018 - today)

LeBron James was drafted in the 2003 draft by the Cleveland Cavaliers with the 1st overall pick. He then moved to the Miami Heat, returned to Cleveland for a second time and now he is a member of the L.A Lakers. His accolades during that period are: 4x NBA Champion, 4x NBA Finals MVP, 4x NBA MVP and 1x Rookie of the year. He was a 18x All Star and a 2x All-Star Game MVP. He was also selected in 13 All-NBA First Teams, 3x All-NBA Second team and an 1x All-NBA Third team.

Los Angeles Lakers (1996-2016)

Kobe Bryant was drafted in the 1996 draft by the Charlotte Hornets with the 13th overall pick and immediately traded to the L.A Lakers . His accolades during that period are: 5x NBA Champion, 2x NBA Finals MVP, 2x NBA MVP. He was a 14x All Star and a 3x All-Star Game MVP. He was also selected in 11 All-NBA First teams, 2 All-NBA Second teams and an 2 All-NBA Third teams.
